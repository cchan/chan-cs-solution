1. Come up with an optimal solution (by hand) for breaking a string of $20$ units after the characters $2$, $8$, and $10$.

    I'm not sure how to prove optimality in this situation without bruteforcing, so here we go. Let's label the break after character $2$ as A, after $8$ as B, and after $10$ as C.

    • $ABC \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 18 + 12 = 50$
    • $ACB \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 18 + 8 = 46$
    • $BAC \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 8 + 12 = 40$
    • $BCA \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 12 + 8 = 40$
    • $CAB \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 10 + 8 = 38$
    • $CBA \Rightarrow \text{ costs } 20 + 10 + 8 = 38$

    So the cheapest way to do it is to break at C first, then A and B in either order (same cost).
