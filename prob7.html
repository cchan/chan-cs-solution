  1. Fill in the table, then compute m[1, 4] using the equation in Problem 5. Show the parentheses for the optimal solution. 
             m[1,4]
     m[1,3]  m[2,4]
   40      80      60
0       0       0      0

    As previously determined, $m[1,3] = 140$, with $(A_1 \times A_2) \times A_3$. Also, $m[2,4] = 84$, with $A_2 \times (A_3 \times A_4)$

             m[1,4]
       140     84
   40      80      60
0       0       0      0

    We recall that $$ m[i,j] = \begin{cases} 0 & i = j \\ \min\limits_{i \leq k < j} \{m[i, k] + m[k + 1, j] + p_{i-1} \cdot p_k \cdot p_j\} & i < j \end{cases} $$ and $p_i=\{5 4 2 10 3\}$ for $i \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$.

    Thus $m[1,4] = \min\limits_{k \in \{1,2,3\}}(m[1,k] + m[k+1,4] + p_0 \cdot p_k \cdot p_4)$.

    For $k=1$ the expression is $m[1,1] + m[2,4] + p_0 \cdot p_1 \cdot p_4 = 0 + 84 + 5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 = 144$.

    For $k=2$ the expression is $m[1,2] + m[3,4] + p_0 \cdot p_2 \cdot p_4 = 40 + 60 + 5 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 = 130$.

    For $k=3$ the expression is $m[1,3] + m[4,4] + p_0 \cdot p_3 \cdot p_4 = 140 + 0 + 5 \cdot 10 \cdot 3 = 290$.

    The answer is thus $\min(144,130,290) = \boxed{130}$. This answer represents the "multiplication" of m[1,2] and m[3,4], so the parenthetical expression would be $\boxed{(A_1 \times A_2) \times (A_3 \times A_4)}$.
