  1. Describe a recursive solution for this problem. Explain what overlapping subproblems arise in the recursive solution.

    Let $L$ be the total length of the string. Let $N$ be the total number of segments to break into. Let $B_i$ be the position of the $i$th break, with $B_0 = 0$ and $B_N = L$.

    Let $C[i,j]$ ($1 \leq i < j \leq N$) be the minimum cost of breaking the segment from $B_i$ to $B_j$ into its constituent pieces.

    $$ C[i,j] = \begin{cases} 0 & i = j - 1 \\ \min\limits_{i < k < j} \{C[i, k] + C[k, j] \} + B_j - B_i & i < j - 1 \end{cases} $$

    A great many overlapping problems arise in a tree-ish structure very similar to the matrix multiplication problem. For example, calculating either $C[1,3]$ or $C[2,4]$ requires $C[2][3]$. Calculating either $C[1,4]$, $C[2,5]$, or $C[3,6]$ all requires $C[3,4]$.

                C[0,4]
        C[0,3]  C[1,4]
    C[0,2]  C[1,3]  C[2,4]
C[0,1]  C[1,2]  C[2,3]  C[3,4]

    It is nearly identical to that of Problem 7.
