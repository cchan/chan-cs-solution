  1. $$ \begin{align*} A_1 &: 5 \times 4\\ A_2 &: 4 \times 2\\ A_3 &: 2 \times 10\\ A_4 &: 10 \times 3 \end{align*} $$ Suppose one performs the multiplications left-to-right: $((A_1 \times A_2) \times A_3) \times A_4$. How many scalar multiplications would be performed?

    Since the number of scalar multiplications is $n \cdot m \cdot k$:

    • $A_1 \times A_2$ gives values of $n=5$, $m=4$, $k=2$, and so $n \cdot m \cdot k = 40$.
    • Multiplying by $A_3$ gives $n \cdot m \cdot k = 5 \cdot 2 \cdot 10 = 100$.
    • Multiplying by $A_4$ gives $n \cdot m \cdot k = 5 \cdot 10 \cdot 3 = 150$.

    Thus the total number of scalar multiplications required is $40 + 100 + 150 = 290$.

  2. How many scalar multiplications are performed when the multiplication proceeds right-to-left: $A_1 \times (A_2 \times (A_3 \times A_4))$?

    This is done quite similarly, except for different values of $n$, $m$, and $k$.

    • $A_3 \times A_4 \Rightarrow 2 \cdot 10 \cdot 3 = 60$.
    • $A_2 \times (A_3 \times A_4) \Rightarrow 4 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 = 24$.
    • $A_3 \times (A_2 \times (A_3 \times A_4)) \Rightarrow 5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 = 60$.

    $\sum = 144$.
